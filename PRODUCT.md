# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Parents and educators who read classical virtue stories aloud to children, often at bedtime or during homeschool lessons. They arrive on a phone or tablet, frequently one-handed, and want to find a story quickly, trust its moral framing, and either read it aloud or play the narrated audio. Secondary readers are adults browsing the virtue descriptions for their own reflection.

## Product Purpose

Classical Virtues presents timeless fables and stories organized around classical virtues (courage, temperance, honesty, and the like), each with a summary, full text, virtue explanation, and narrated audio. Success looks like a parent finding the right story in under a minute and the reading experience feeling like a well-bound book, not a website.

## Brand Personality

Timeless, warm, literary. The voice of a trusted family anthology: classical gravitas softened by approachable warmth. Reading a story should feel calm and unhurried; browsing should feel like leafing through a beautiful book.

## Anti-references

- Startup landing pages: no hero metrics, gradient CTAs, or SaaS grammar.
- Kids' cartoon sites: no bouncy mascots, primary-color confetti, or juvenile typography.
- Content-farm blogs: no ad-shaped layouts, listicle energy, or SEO-first copy.

## Design Principles

1. **The book is the interface.** Typography, rhythm, and white space carry the brand; chrome stays out of the story's way.
2. **One-handed, read-aloud first.** A parent holding a phone in one hand and a child in the other is the primary scene; controls must be reachable and forgiving.
3. **Earn trust through restraint.** Calm pacing, honest copy, and consistent structure signal editorial care; nothing flashes or sells.
4. **Every story stands alone.** Each story page must work as a landing page from search: clear virtue context, obvious audio, no dependence on prior navigation.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA: 4.5:1 minimum text contrast, full keyboard operability (especially the audio player), visible focus states, reduced-motion alternatives, meaningful alt text, and screen-reader-announced state changes for audio playback.
